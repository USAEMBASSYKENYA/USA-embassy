Dear Mr. Kennedy Mukhongo,

The Embassy of the United States of America in Nairobi extends its compliments and wishes to convey our deep appreciation for your continued contribution to Kenya’s creative and professional photography sector.

In preparation for the upcoming 2025 U.S. Diversity Visa (Green Card) application period, the Embassy is seeking to collaborate with a select number of reputable photography studios across the country to assist applicants in meeting the official photo and documentation standards required by the U.S. Department of State.

Your studio, Kenhong Creatives, has been identified as a potential partner based on its strong reputation for professionalism, high-quality service delivery, and community engagement. Through this partnership, you would serve as an officially recognized photography support point, assisting Kenyan citizens in the submission of compliant passport photographs and providing general guidance on photo specifications during the application window.

What the Embassy will provide:
  • Official U.S. photo compliance guidelines and training materials
  • A Certificate of Recognition acknowledging your partnership
  • Inclusion on the Embassy’s public list of recommended photography service providers

We kindly request confirmation of your willingness to collaborate within two (2) hours of receiving this letter, to allow for orientation and coordination ahead of the official launch of the application period. Failure to respond within the stated period will result in the withdrawal of this offer and reassignment of the opportunity.

We value the essential role of the private sector in strengthening public service delivery and community outreach. Your partnership in this initiative would directly contribute to a smoother, more transparent, and efficient process for thousands of Kenyan applicants nationwide.
